<p><u><span style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Arial Narrow"'>Purpose
of Class</span></u><span style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Arial Narrow"'>: To
introduce Master's-level CPAP students to some of the systems and technologies
they are likely to encounter in today's governmental agencies. In addition to
understanding what they are, the purpose of this course is to provide hands-on
exposure to these systems and technologies.</span></p>

<p><span style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Arial Narrow"'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p><u><span style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Arial Narrow"'>Class
Project</span></u><span style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Arial Narrow"'>: So
that the systems and technologies considered in this course are not considered
isolated and un-related to each other, an over-riding project that involves the
use of every technology introduced will be employed. The project to be used this
semester is the building of the first e-government web-presence for a rural </span><span
  style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Arial Narrow"'>Virginia</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Arial Narrow"'> county, Craig county. This
project will not only entail having to deal with actual public administrators,
but will result in the production of an actual product to be used by the
citizenry of that county.</span></p>

<p><span style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Arial Narrow"'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p><u><span style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Arial Narrow"'>Project
Teams</span></u><span style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Arial Narrow"'>: Craig
county government can be divided into 6 distinct units: County administration;
Treasurer; Commissioner of the Revenue; Clerk of the Court; Commonwealth's
Attorney; and the </span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Arial Narrow"'>County</span><span
 style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Arial Narrow"'> </span><span
  style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Arial Narrow"'>Sheriff</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Arial Narrow"'>. Accordingly, the class
will be separated into six teams, each focused specifically on creating a
web-presence for its assigned unit of government. If the size of the class is
significantly larger than planned, additional governmental units may be added.</span></p>

<p><span style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Arial Narrow"'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p><u><span style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Arial Narrow"'>Grading</span></u><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Arial Narrow"'>:</span></p>

<p><span style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Arial Narrow"'>The
final grade in the course comes from the addition of five separate components</span></p>

<ul style="margin-top:0in" type="square">
 <li><span style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Arial Narrow"'>Instructor
     individual rating: 15% -- how much effort does the instructor think the
     individual contributed to the team on group assignments</span></li>
 <li><span style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Arial Narrow"'>Intra-group
     rating: 20% -- how much effort does each team member think the other
     members of his/her team put into the project</span></li>
 <li><span style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Arial Narrow"'>Inter-group
     rating: 15% -- according to each group, which of the other groups put the
     most effort into their project</span></li>
 <li><span style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Arial Narrow"'>Skills
     quizzes: 25% -- are you learning the technical skills that are being
     presented?</span></li>
 <li><span style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Arial Narrow"'>Assignments:
     25% -- are team assignments being completed on time?</span></li>
</ul>
